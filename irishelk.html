The Irish elk (Megaloceros giganteus), also called the giant deer, was an extinct species of deer that lived during the Pleistocene epoch, from about 2.6 million to 11,700 years ago. It was one of the largest deer that ever lived, standing up to 2.1 meters (6 feet 11 inches) tall at the shoulders and weighing up to 700 kilograms (1,540 pounds). It is believed to have lived in open woodlands and grasslands. The Irish elk was likely a social animal, and it may have lived in herds.The Irish elk became extinct at the end of the Pleistocene epoch.
